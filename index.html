Niche Information and the Emergence of Conventions: A Large Group Behavioral Experiment

This chart depicts the core results of a behavioral experiment with two independent variables. The participants attempted to interact using the same name for an object, here a pictured person. If participants matched names they received $.10. If they did not, they lost $.05 (if they had that much). There was no constraint on the possible names and no incentive for matching with the whole group. The experiment ran for 25 rounds, with a new round at least every 15 seconds. The outcome of interest was whether the group discovered a single name to use, a group convention.

Each colored square represents the results of a single trial with the treatment variables identified by the row and column of the table. Please click on the squares to see the visualization of that trial. The design of the experiment reflects the desire to understand what information treatments, controlled by the shape of the network participants were embedded in and the number of additional names they are shown (i.e. niche information), allow the group to discover a single name for the object. A square is green when the group discovered a single name and red when not. Most treatment cells received four trials, but for reasons discussed elsewhere not all did. Those absent trials are noted as gray squares.

The network topology treatment can be a fully connected graph, a random graph with constant degree 4), a small world graph with constant degree 4, or a lattice graph with degree 4.The niche information/name count treatment variable has counts 0, 1, and 2. For non-zero treatments, participants are shown names was played by someone in the group in the previous round but the viewing participant does not know who played it. The names are chosen at random and therefore are often different for different participants. This treament variable is the variable of theoretical interest for the project because this type of information is often neglected in social network dynamics research.
